### On the balance of synthetic and real data in training language models

TinyStories ([Eldan & Li, 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.07759)) shows that a small, high-quality dataset of simple English stories can be used to train a language model that

- is smaller than the state-of-the-art models (below 10 million total parameters), or have much simpler architectures (with only one transformer block)
- can generate coherent and diverse stories with a high degree of grammatical correctness

However, it's known that the synthetic dataset (generated by GPT-4) used for is [not simple text as claimed and contains nonsense words](https://huggingface.co/datasets/roneneldan/TinyStories/discussions/18). Furthermore, training on model-generated content might lead to model collaps where the output of the new language model degrades; worse, lexical, syntactic, and semantic diversity tend to decrease ([Shumailov et al., 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.17493); [Guo et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2311.09807)). Instead of relying on synthetic data, accummulating real and synthetic data can help avoid model collapse ([Gerstgrasser, et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01413)).
